A fourth wave of U.S. strikes hits Iran as oil jumps and Hormuz traffic collapses to a trickle

CENTCOM launched a fourth wave of strikes on Iran overnight, hitting dozens of new targets and using one-way attack drones at sea for the first time, days after the IRGC set the Cyprus-flagged tanker MV GFS Galaxy ablaze in the strait and left a crew member missing. Iran answered with drone and missile strikes on U.S.-linked sites in Bahrain, Kuwait and Jordan and declared passage through Hormuz "not possible" — CENTCOM insists traffic is "flowing." Brent crude jumped more than 4% to $79 a barrel, and just six vessels crossed the strait between Thursday and Friday, down from 18-22 a day earlier this month.
